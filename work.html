Dancing with the Stars: The Mirrorball's Destiny

2026 Mathematical Contest In Modeling (MCM) | 01/2026 - 02/2026

+

Investigating the conflict between professional elitism and mass public appeal in reality TV voting systems.

Visual Asset Project Overview Diagram

Overview

Context

Dancing with the Stars struggles with the conflict between expert technical scoring and subjective fan voting, often leading to controversial eliminations.

Objective

To develop a mathematical framework that estimates hidden fan voting data, evaluates past scoring rules, and proposes a fairer voting mechanism.

Visual Asset SHAP Attribution Chart

Methodologies

  • PL-PECON (Latent Preference Decoding): Used Monte Carlo Rejection Sampling to convert fan votes into a high-dimensional constrained inverse problem, establishing a dynamic rationality upper bound $\gamma \approx 2.3$ to successfully reproduce historical elimination lists with a 96.73% consistency rate.
  • PRIME-AMC (Mechanism Comparison): Deployed a Probabilistic Reconstruction & Information-Markov Evaluation model using Absorbing Markov Chains to simulate counterfactual competition trajectories.
  • DSRF-SHAP (Factor Decoupling): Implemented a Dual-Stream Random Forest with SHAP to decode voting drivers, revealing that judges prioritize technical proficiency (partner synergy contributes >35%).
  • SARF (System Optimization): Proposed a Sigmoid-Gated Adaptive Rank Fusion system maintaining high rank-skill correlation (Spearman 0.9-1.0).
Visual Asset Ranking vs Percentage Method Comparison

Impact & Skills

Key Finding

The percentage method is biased toward fan votes (high popularity overrides professional scores when dispersion exceeds 0.6), whereas the ranking method suppresses extreme variance and maintains a high SNR of 0.96.

Strategic Recommendation

Submitted a memorandum recommending a hybrid strategy combining the ranking method with the "Judges' Save" mechanism, paired with a dynamic gating strategy.

Hainan Tourism Research: Predicting High-Engagement Guides

2025 "Academic Excellence Climbing Program" | 11/2025

+

A data-driven analysis to uncover the core features of highly "liked" user-generated travel content on Qunar.

Visual Asset UGC Engagement Analysis

Overview

Context

While User-Generated Content (UGC) shapes modern travel decisions, content creators and platforms struggle to identify what truly drives user engagement and high "likes" for Hainan tourism guides.

Objective

To build a robust predictive model using machine learning and NLP that identifies the key factors driving high engagement, providing actionable strategies for content creators and platform algorithms.

Visual Asset LDA Topic Map

Methodologies

  • Semantic Analysis & NLP: Applied text mining and LDA (Latent Dirichlet Allocation) topic modeling to extract structured features from unstructured travel guides, identifying distinct thematic clusters like "Hainan natural landscapes."
  • Quantile Regression Analysis: Utilized quantile regression to analyze the non-linear impact of "per capita consumption" on engagement, revealing a negative correlation for low-to-mid engagement guides.
  • Decision Tree Classification: Built and optimized a Decision Tree classifier (incorporating Grid Search for hyperparameter tuning) to predict high-engagement likelihood.
Visual Asset Feature Importance Chart

Impact & Skills

Key Finding

Feature importance analysis revealed that mentioning "Shopping" (0.550) and "Food" (0.160) are the strongest predictors of high engagement. Guides tailored to "Family" or "Friends" trips generated significantly higher interactions.

Strategic Recommendation

Formulated actionable content creation formulas (e.g., combining in-depth travel, food, and photography elements for group trips) to maximize user engagement.

Machine Learning NLP (LDA) Quantile Regression Business Analytics

Zhejiang Entrepreneurship & Organizational Resilience

ZJSU College Students Innovation & Entrepreneurship Training Program | 04/2025 - Present

+

Exploring the impact of regional business culture on corporate green transformation and resilience through mixed methods.

Visual Asset Green Transformation Context

Overview

Context

In the face of economic transitions and the "Dual Carbon" goals, traditional manufacturing enterprises face severe challenges in green transformation.

Objective

To investigate how the "Zhejiang Entrepreneurship Spirit" (Zheshang Spirit) acts as an informal institution to drive green value creation and enhance organizational resilience.

Visual Asset APCC Dynamic Model

Methodologies

  • Empirical Analysis (SEM & IPA): Collected survey data from 264 startups. Utilized Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) to quantify impacts of entrepreneurial spirit on organizational resilience.
  • Longitudinal Case Study (APCC Model): Conducted an in-depth study of Hangzhou Iron & Steel Group's 10-year green transformation. Developed the "APCC" dynamic model to decode resource orchestration.
  • Field Research & Text Mining: Conducted over 5 hours of interviews with senior management, generating a 60,000-word transcript with multi-level coding analysis.
Visual Asset Resilience Pathways Diagram

Impact & Skills

Key Finding

Validated that regional entrepreneurial spirit significantly enhances organizational resilience through four paths: boosting risk-taking, promoting cooperation, easing financial constraints, and strengthening responsibility.

Recognition

The case study report won the First Prize in the 11th Zhejiang Provincial University Student Economic Case Analysis Competition (University Level).
