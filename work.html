01

Dancing with the Stars: The Mirrorball's Destiny

2026 Mathematical Contest In Modeling (MCM) | 01/2026 - 02/2026

+

Investigating the conflict between professional elitism and mass public appeal in reality TV voting systems.

Overview & Background

Context

The show "Dancing with the Stars" struggles with the conflict between expert technical scoring and subjective fan voting, often leading to controversial elimination results.

Objective

To develop a mathematical model that estimates hidden fan voting data, evaluates past scoring rules, and proposes a fairer voting mechanism.

Role

As the core modeler on the team, I led the design and code implementation of the underlying algorithmic framework. I was responsible for building a vote estimation model using Monte Carlo algorithms and translating complex audience psychology into quantifiable, interpretable mathematical features through Random Forest and SHAP attribution analysis in machine learning.

Core Methodologies

  • PL-PECON: Used Monte Carlo rejection sampling to decode latent fan preferences, achieving a 96.73% historical consistency rate.
  • PRIME-AMC: Used Markov Chains to evaluate and contrast the scoring effects of ranking versus percentage methods.
  • DSRF-SHAP: Combined SHAP with a Dual-Stream Random Forest model to decouple voting drivers.
  • SARF: A Sigmoid-gated adaptive rank fusion that conditionally activates fan votes based on technical thresholds.

Research Findings

Key Findings

When a percentage-based voting method is adopted, there is a severe bias towards fan votes. Conversely, the ranking method effectively suppresses extreme variance and maintains a high signal-to-noise ratio of 0.96.

Strategic Recommendation

Recommended a hybrid strategy combining the ranking method with a "Judges' Save" mechanism, supplemented by dynamic gating, to ensure the trophy truly represents dancing excellence.

02

Hainan Tourism Research: Predicting High-Engagement Guides

2025 "Academic Excellence Climbing Program" | 11/2025

+

Revealing the core characteristics of highly-liked user travel guides on Qunar.com through data-driven analysis.

Overview & Background

Context

Although highly-liked travel guides on the internet profoundly influence modern travel planning, it is difficult for both content creators and platforms to accurately identify what factors drive this high engagement.

Objective

To build predictive models using machine learning and natural language processing techniques, identify key drivers of "likes," and provide actionable strategies.

Role

As the lead data analyst, I independently completed the entire data mining process, from unstructured text processing to feature engineering. I proficiently utilized NLP techniques to clean and parse massive amounts of text, and built Decision Tree and Quantile Regression models to transform discrete text content into predictive metrics.

Core Methodologies

  • Semantic Analysis & NLP: Applied text mining and LDA topic modeling to extract structured features from unstructured travelogues.
  • Quantile Regression: Analyzed the non-linear impact of per capita consumption, revealing a negative correlation in low-to-medium engagement content, but an insignificant impact in top-tier, highly-liked content.
  • Decision Tree Classification: Built and optimized a classifier using grid search to predict the likelihood of high engagement based on dimensions such as travel group type, attractions, and number of photos.

Research Findings

Key Findings

"Shopping" (0.550) and "Food" (0.160) are the strongest predictors, with weights far exceeding the mere mention of popular beaches. Furthermore, travelogues featuring "family" or "friends" traveling together have higher engagement rates.

Strategic Recommendation

Formulated a content creation formula (e.g., combining in-depth tours, food, and photography for group travel) to maximize engagement rates and optimize the platform's recommendation algorithms.

03

Zhejiang Entrepreneurship & Organizational Resilience

ZJSU College Students Innovation & Entrepreneurship Training Program | 04/2025 - Present

+

Employed a mixed-methods approach, utilizing survey data from 264 startups and in-depth qualitative interviews, to deeply explore how the "Zhejiang Spirit" empowers organizational resilience in enterprises.

Overview & Background

Context

Amidst a complex macroeconomic environment, existing research primarily focuses on formal institutional support, rarely addressing the profound impact of informal cultural systems—such as regional business spirit—on a company's ability to withstand risks.

Objective

To decode the core essence of the Zhejiang Spirit, unveil its internal mechanisms for enhancing entrepreneurial resilience through Structural Equation Modeling, and distill a replicable "Zhejiang Experience" to guide entrepreneurial practices nationwide.

Role

I was deeply involved in field research and data analysis. I performed multi-level coding on 60,000 words of in-depth interview transcripts, achieving a perfect integration of data models and business narratives.

Core Methodologies

  • Empirical Analysis: Conducted a questionnaire survey of 264 startups. Used Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) to quantify impact paths, and applied Importance-Performance Analysis (IPA) to process interview texts.
  • Longitudinal Case Study: Conducted an in-depth analysis of Hangang Group's 10-year transition journey, constructing the APCC (Antecedents-Process-Consequences-Chinese Context) model.
  • Field Research: Conducted over 5 hours of in-depth interviews, compiled 60,000 words of verbatim transcripts, and used multi-level coding for qualitative comprehensive analysis.

Research Findings

Key Findings

Validated that regional spirit can enhance corporate resilience by stimulating a willingness to take risks, promoting cooperation, alleviating financing constraints, and strengthening a sense of responsibility.

Strategic Recommendation

Proposed a synergistic strategy combining an "efficient market" with a "proactive government" for policymakers, providing a roadmap for the transformation of the traditional manufacturing industry.
